Frequently Asked Questions

What is Cyber Law?

Cyber Law is a set of rules that protect people while they use the internet. It helps keep personal information safe and prevents online crimes like hacking and identity theft.

What are common types of cybercrimes?

Common cybercrimes include hacking, stealing personal information, online scams, spreading viruses, and bullying or harassing people online.

What is Data Privacy?

Data privacy means keeping personal information safe and secure. Cyber Laws make sure companies protect your data and ask for permission before collecting it.

What should I do if I am scammed online?

You should report it to the cybercrime police or use government websites made for reporting online fraud. They will investigate and take action.

Is hacking illegal?

Yes, hacking is illegal. If someone hacks into another person’s computer or account, they can be fined or sent to jail..

How can I report a cybercrime?

You can report it at your local police station or on official websites set up for reporting cybercrimes.

Are there rules for using social media?

Yes, Cyber Laws say you must not post hateful content, steal someone’s work, spread fake news, or misuse someone’s private information.

Is it illegal to download movies or music for free?

Yes, downloading movies, music, or software without paying for them or without permission is illegal. It’s called piracy.
